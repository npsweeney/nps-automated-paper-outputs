# Worker-Firm Sorting in Training Subsidies Policy

Subject: `labour_economics`

## Contribution Seed

This worker output turns the farm idea into a concrete paper seed. The proposed contribution is to identify where the current literature leaves a measurable gap around `Worker-Firm Sorting in Training Subsidies Policy`, then pair that gap with auditable data and methods before escalating to a full manuscript.

## Data Plan

Use worker panels, employer-employee matched data, vacancy postings, wage records, commuting zones, occupation and skill taxonomies. Raw downloads should remain temporary; public output should keep only manifests, derived summaries, and reproducible instructions.

## Methods Plan

Candidate methods: shift-share designs, worker-firm fixed effects, event studies, hazard models, search-and-matching diagnostics, heterogeneous treatment effects. The next stage must choose the strongest design justified by the data rather than defaulting to a fashionable model.
